Open Data for Open Lands

The federal government has released a revised draft contract that will define how we access our public land for the next decade. They are seeking a private contractor to build Recreation.gov, which will provide online access to our nationâ€™s parks, forests, monuments, campsites, cabins, and tours. While great progress has been made, the government needs to take a stand and define the key elements that will create a platform for innovation.

We are advocating for a more open system, where open data and APIs empower many applications to help people connect with their parks. These market forces will create better options for the public while also reaching new and diverse demographics, the key to inspiring the next generation of conservationists.

As a result of this coalition's efforts, the revised draft has come a long way - but it is still missing critical requirements. Help us encourage open data for our public lands by submitting your comment today!

Example Comment

Thank you for your hard work and great progress in the new draft RFP for Recreation.gov, especially around real-time availability data and third party sales. These improvements are great, but they do not go far enough.

To create a Recreation.gov that will function as an effective platform, the RFP must guarantee a financial incentive for third party applications by setting a commission guideline for facilitating bookings. The RFP must also require technology that can process bookings from within third party applications.

These critical changes are needed to encourage the innovation that will inspire the next generation of outdoor enthusiasts and conservationists

I urge to make these changes before releasing the final draft. Thank you for your consideration and hard work.
