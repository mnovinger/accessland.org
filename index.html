Open Data for Open Lands

The federal government is requesting feedback on a draft contract that will define how we access our public lands for the next decade. This contract is for Recreation.gov, the website that provides access to our nation’s parks, forests, campsites, cabins, and tours. As drafted, this contract places all the revenue and bookings into the hands of one contractor and one website, creating a closed silo and a monopoly.

We can create a better world by encouraging the government to require a more open system. We've successfully pushed to extend the comment period until November 30th and have the attention of the Whitehouse and Congress. We need your help in sending the message that this contract needs to require open data and an API, which will allow third parties to build on top of Recreation.gov, improving access to public lands.

Example Comments

Example 1: President Obama has made it clear that he wants to “make sure that we’re giving entrepreneurs the ability, if we build an effective platform, to essentially develop apps that work off this new information.” To follow through with that promise, I encourage you to require an API for public land data that includes availability and facilitates bookings. Anything less would be a big step backward and out of step with the administration’s open data policy.
Example 2: You can book a hotel using everything from Kayak to TripAdvisor to the Hilton website. So why in 2014 do we have only one choice for where to book a campsite or cabin on our public lands? The travel industry has proven that allowing for distribution creates better experiences for the customers and increases sales. Our parks deserve the same. Please require open data and a bookings API in this contract so that we can harness the creativity of private sector for the benefit of the outdoor community. A government contracted monopoly will not do the same.
